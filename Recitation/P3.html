What is a byte?

A byte is a group of 8 binary digits(1 or 0). Bytes serves as a measurement of sizes of programs or files, but it's also has other uses such as keeping track of relative information.

Here are some examples of bytes and its corresponding decimal value:

  • 0000 0001 = '1'
  • 0000 1111 = '15'
  • 1000 0000 = '128'
  • 1111 1111 = '255'

The highest number a byte can keep track of is '255'. Anything higher would require another byte to keep track of its decimal value.
